# Example Garden Plan: Tomato Variety Trial

Testing different tomato varieties for taste, yield, and disease resistance.

## Varieties Setup

### Parent: Tomato
- Abbreviation: TOM
- Days to Maturity: 70 (default)
- Color: Red (#ef4444)

### Varieties to Add:
1. **Cherry Tomato**
   - Abbreviation: CHE
   - Days: 60 (10 days earlier)
   - Color: Bright Red
   
2. **Beefsteak**
   - Abbreviation: BEE
   - Days: 85 (15 days later)
   - Color: Deep Red
   
3. **Roma**
   - Abbreviation: ROM
   - Days: 75 (5 days later)
   - Color: Red (inherit)
   
4. **Yellow Pear**
   - Abbreviation: YEL
   - Days: 70 (same as default)
   - Color: Yellow (#eab308)

## Garden Layout

### Raised Bed 1: "Tomato Trial Bed"
All tomatoes: 1 plant per square foot

- **Row 1**: 
  - Squares 1-3: Cherry Tomato
  - Squares 4-6: Yellow Pear
  
- **Row 2**:
  - Squares 1-3: Roma
  - Squares 4-6: Beefsteak
  
- **Row 3**:
  - Squares 1-6: Cherry Tomato (succession - plant 2 weeks later)

## Planting Schedule

**First Planting** (May 1):
- Cherry Tomato (Row 1)
- Yellow Pear (Row 1)
- Roma (Row 2)
- Beefsteak (Row 2)

**Second Planting** (May 15):
- Cherry Tomato (Row 3) - for extended harvest

## Expected Harvest Dates

From May 1 planting:
- Cherry: June 30 (60 days)
- Yellow Pear: July 10 (70 days)
- Roma: July 15 (75 days)
- Beefsteak: July 25 (85 days)

From May 15 planting:
- Cherry: July 14 (60 days)

## Support Needed

All tomatoes need:
- Cages or stakes (6ft tall minimum)
- Pruning for indeterminate varieties
- Regular watering
- Mulch to prevent soil splash

## Notes to Track

For each variety, record:
- [ ] First ripe fruit date
- [ ] Total yield (pounds)
- [ ] Taste rating (1-10)
- [ ] Disease resistance
- [ ] Plant vigor
- [ ] Favorite uses

## Comparison Goals

**Cherry**: 
- Best for: Snacking, salads
- Expected yield: High

**Beefsteak**:
- Best for: Slicing, burgers
- Expected yield: Medium (larger fruits)

**Roma**:
- Best for: Sauce, canning
- Expected yield: High

**Yellow Pear**:
- Best for: Unique color, salads
- Expected yield: Medium-high

## Tips

- Start indoors 6-8 weeks before last frost
- Harden off for 1 week before transplanting
- Plant deep - bury stem up to first true leaves
- Add calcium at planting (prevent blossom end rot)
- Water consistently (1-2 inches per week)
